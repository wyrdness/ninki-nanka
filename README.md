## Overview

The Ninki Nanka ('dragon' or 'great serpent' in Mandinka) is a long-standing legendary creature of Gambian, Senegalese, and broader West African folklore, said to inhabit the swamps, rivers, and mangrove forests of the lower Gambia. Descriptions vary widely: some accounts give a crocodile-like head with a horse- or donkey-like body, others describe a giraffe-necked, three-horned beast roughly 30 feet (9 m) long with reflective scales and a fatal stare. Among the Mandinka, merely seeing the Ninki Nanka is said to bring death within weeks, making it one of West Africa's most feared supernatural figures. The creature occupies a dual role as both guardian spirit and deadly threat. In summer 2006 the Centre for Fortean Zoology mounted the J. T. Downes Memorial Gambia Expedition, led by Richard Freeman, to collect Gambian eyewitness testimony; no specimen was recovered, but the expedition documented a consistent body of recent sightings and was covered by the BBC.

## Notable Sightings

### CFZ Gambia Expedition (2006) ⭐
> **Date**: 2006-07-01  
> **Location**: Lower Gambia River and surrounding villages  
> **Witnesses**: 0 (Gambian villagers and elders interviewed by the expedition)  
The Centre for Fortean Zoology's J. T. Downes Memorial Gambia Expedition, led by Richard Freeman, conducted interviews and field investigation along the lower Gambia in summer 2006. Informants described the Ninki Nanka variously as a long-bodied reptile, a 'Chinese dragon'-like creature, and a winged fire-spitting serpent. No specimen, photograph, or physical trace was recovered.

## Theories & Explanations

### Paranormal / Cryptozoological

**Guardian / dragon spirit** _(Mandinka and Senegambian oral tradition)_

Within Mandinka and Senegambian tradition the Ninki Nanka is a real, agentive spirit-being whose territory is dangerous to humans.

### Scientific

**Surviving relict reptile (cryptozoological)** _(Richard Freeman, Centre for Fortean Zoology)_

Cryptozoologists including Richard Freeman have argued the Ninki Nanka could represent a surviving large reptile, possibly an undescribed monitor lizard or relict crocodilian / sauropod-like animal.

### Skeptical

**Misidentification and folklore** _(Mainstream zoological consensus)_

Sightings are best explained as misidentification of large reptiles (Nile crocodile, monitor lizard, rock python) interpreted through a strong cultural template.
